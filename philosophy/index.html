Philosophy — Conceptions of Reality

The foundational graph: how thinkers have conceived of what reality fundamentally is, from Plato’s cave to the quantum measurement problem. Not a timeline — a web of influence, reaction, and synthesis. Seven of these thinkers ground the EOS operators.

Ancient & Hellenistic

  • Ancient - Parmenides, Heraclitus, Pythagoras, Plato, Aristotle, the Atomists, Epicurus, the Stoics, Plotinus, Sextus Empiricus

Medieval & Rationalist

  • Medieval / Rationalist - Augustine, Avicenna, Averroes, al-Ghazali, Anselm, Aquinas, Duns Scotus, Ockham, Descartes, Malebranche, Spinoza, Leibniz

Empiricism & the Critical Turn

  • Empiricism / Kant - Bacon, Hobbes, Locke, Berkeley, Hume, Reid, Kant (grounds O1_FOUNDATION)

Nineteenth & Twentieth Century

  • 19th–20th century - Hegel, Schopenhauer, Marx, Nietzsche, Mach, Peirce, James, Bergson, Husserl, Whitehead, Russell, Wittgenstein (grounds O3), Heidegger, Quine (grounds O4), Putnam

Poststructuralist & STS

  • Poststructuralist - Latour (grounds O4), Foucault (grounds O5), Deleuze (grounds O3 + dynamics), Derrida (grounds O5)

Physics of Reality

  • Physics of reality - Newton, Einstein, Bohr, Heisenberg, Schrödinger, Everett, Bohm, Bell, and the Measurement Problem
