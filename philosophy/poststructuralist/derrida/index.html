The model of reality

Derrida denies that any concept rests on a final, fixed ground of presence. Meaning arises from différance — a movement that is at once difference and deferral: a sign means only by differing from other signs and by postponing any final settling of sense. There is no “outside the text” that would halt this play and anchor meaning once and for all. Stable reference is thus always already unstable.

Key concept

Deconstruction; différance; the critique of the “metaphysics of presence”; “il n’y a pas de hors-texte”.

Relations

Derrida is influenced by Husserl’s phenomenology (which he reads against itself), by Heidegger’s destruction of metaphysics, and by Nietzsche’s assault on fixed truth.

Grounds

Derrida grounds the radical dimension of O5_DE_REFERENTIALIZATION — the most dangerous operator, which removes the stable reference point of a concept. Necessary for expansion but destructive without prior grounding: in EOS deconstruction is a tool, not a worldview, and is never applied without O1_FOUNDATION first.
