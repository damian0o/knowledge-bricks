## The model of reality
For Latour there is no clean divide between subjects (knowers) and objects (the known).
Reality is assembled from networks in which humans, tools, instruments, texts, and
institutions are all *actors* (actants) that hold each other in place. What we call a
"fact" or an "object" is the stabilized effect of a web of associations; dissolve the
network and the object loses its solidity. Agency is therefore not a uniquely human
property — it is distributed across the whole assembly.

## Key concept
Actor-Network Theory (ANT); the symmetry of human and non-human actors; "follow the actors".

## Relations
Latour **reacts against** the representational, subject-centred epistemology inherited from
the Cartesian and Kantian tradition, which treats the knowing mind as separate from a passive
world of objects. His relational picture overlaps with the holism running from
[Quine's web of belief](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/quine.md): no node stands alone.

## Grounds
Latour grounds [O4_RELATIONALIZATION](/eos/operators/o4-relationalization.md): in EOS the
concept space includes non-human actors — the codebase, the CI pipeline, the wiki — as equal
nodes in the knowledge net, so transformations apply to the whole actor-network and not to
human intent alone.
