## The model of reality
Derrida denies that any concept rests on a final, fixed ground of presence. Meaning arises from
**différance** — a movement that is at once difference and deferral: a sign means only by
differing from other signs and by postponing any final settling of sense. There is no
"outside the text" that would halt this play and anchor meaning once and for all. Stable
reference is thus always already unstable.

## Key concept
Deconstruction; différance; the critique of the "metaphysics of presence"; "il n'y a pas de hors-texte".

## Relations
Derrida is **influenced by** [Husserl's phenomenology](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/husserl.md)
(which he reads against itself), by [Heidegger's destruction of metaphysics](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/heidegger.md),
and by [Nietzsche's assault on fixed truth](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/nietzsche.md).
