The model of reality

Deleuze inverts the priority of identity over difference: difference is not a gap between pre-existing things but the productive force from which forms emerge. Reality is a flux of intensities and multiplicities that continually differentiate, organised into provisional assemblages rather than fixed substances. The system never settles into a global equilibrium; local stability coexists with global drift. A converged system, on this view, is a dead one.

Key concept

Difference and repetition; multiplicity; the rhizome and assemblage; productive difference.

Relations

Deleuze is influenced by Nietzsche’s will to power and eternal return, by Bergson’s duration and creative becoming, and by Spinoza’s single expressive substance.

Grounds

Deleuze grounds two parts of EOS. He grounds O3_LINGUISTIC_SHIFT — meaning is generated within shifting fields rather than read off fixed definitions — and he grounds EOS System Dynamics: an open, non-equilibrium system of continuous structural mutation, where local stability is maintained while global drift is expected and healthy.
