## The model of reality
Foucault brackets the question of what reality "really is" and asks instead what makes a
statement count as *knowable* and *true* in a given epoch. Each age has an **episteme** — an
underlying order of discourse that silently fixes what is thinkable. Knowledge and power are
inseparable (*pouvoir-savoir*): the practices that produce truth are the same practices that
discipline and order subjects. Every "best practice" is therefore a dominant discourse, not a
neutral fact.

## Key concept
Power/knowledge (pouvoir-savoir); the episteme; discourse as the condition of the sayable.

## Relations
Foucault is **influenced by** [Nietzsche's perspectivism and genealogy](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/nietzsche.md)
— there are no facts behind appearances, only interpretations backed by force — and by
