## The model of reality
Experience gives us only impressions and the fainter ideas copied from them. We never perceive a necessary connection between cause and effect — only constant conjunction — so causal necessity is a habit of mind, not a feature read off reality. The enduring self, too, dissolves into a bundle of perceptions. What "reality" is in itself lies beyond what reason can secure.

## Key concept
Skepticism about causation (constant conjunction, not necessary connection); Hume's fork (relations of ideas vs. matters of fact); the bundle theory of the self.

## Relations
Hume **refines** and radicalizes the empiricism of [John Locke](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/locke.md) and the critique of [George Berkeley](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/berkeley.md). He **reacts against** rationalism — the innate ideas and rational causal necessity of [René Descartes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md) and [Gottfried Leibniz](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md). He **influenced** [Immanuel Kant](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/kant.md), whom he "awakened from his dogmatic slumber," and is **opposed by** [Thomas Reid](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/reid.md).
