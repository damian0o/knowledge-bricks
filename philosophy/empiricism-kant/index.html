Empiricism & the Critical Turn

The British empiricists drive knowledge back to the senses, each pushing the previous one further, until Hume’s skepticism dissolves causation and the self — and Kant answers by relocating structure into the mind itself.

Concepts

  • Francis Bacon — Inductive Naturalism - Reality is a mind-independent natural order uncovered by inductive experiment.
  • Thomas Hobbes — Corporeal Materialism - Reality is nothing but bodies in motion.
  • John Locke — Empiricism & the Veil of Ideas - A material world known only indirectly through ideas; primary vs. secondary qualities.
  • George Berkeley — Subjective Idealism - There is no matter; to be is to be perceived.
  • David Hume — Skeptical Empiricism - Causation, the self, and the external world cannot be demonstrated by reason.
  • Thomas Reid — Common Sense Realism - We perceive the external world directly; common sense needs no proof.
  • Immanuel Kant — Transcendental Idealism - We know phenomena structured by the mind, never the thing-in-itself. Grounds O1_FOUNDATION.
