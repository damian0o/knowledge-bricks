## The model of reality
Reality is an objective natural order that exists independently of the mind. Its hidden structures — the "forms" or causal laws behind appearances — cannot be reached by deductive speculation but only by methodical observation and inductive generalization from experience.

## Key concept
The inductive method (*Novum Organum*) and the four **Idols** of the mind — the distorting biases (Tribe, Cave, Marketplace, Theatre) that corrupt our grasp of nature.

## Relations
Bacon **reacts against** Aristotelian and Scholastic deduction, rejecting the syllogism as a tool for discovering nature — see [Aristotle](/philosophy/ancient/aristotle.md). He **influenced** [Thomas Hobbes](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/hobbes.md), who served as his secretary, and **influenced** the later empiricism of [John Locke](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/locke.md) and the program of the Royal Society.
