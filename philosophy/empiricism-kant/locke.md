## The model of reality
The world of material substances exists independently of us, but the mind is a *tabula rasa* with no innate ideas; all knowledge is built from experience. We perceive objects only indirectly, via ideas. **Primary qualities** (extension, shape, motion) genuinely belong to bodies; **secondary qualities** (colour, taste) are powers in objects to produce sensations in us.

## Key concept
Tabula rasa (no innate ideas); the distinction between primary and secondary qualities; ideas as the immediate objects of perception.

## Relations
Locke **reacts against** the innatism of [René Descartes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md), and was **influenced by** [Francis Bacon](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/bacon.md) and [Thomas Hobbes](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/hobbes.md); his corpuscularian account refines Robert Boyle's chemistry. He **influenced** [George Berkeley](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/berkeley.md), [David Hume](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/hume.md), and [Immanuel Kant](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/kant.md), who took up the empiricist account of experience as the raw material of cognition.
