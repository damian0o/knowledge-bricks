## The model of reality
We perceive the external world directly, not through a veil of ideas. Belief in a mind-independent reality is a first principle of common sense — foundational, universal, and impossible to coherently doubt. The skeptical conclusions of the "way of ideas" are taken as a *reductio* of its starting premise rather than as truths about reality.

## Key concept
Common-sense realism and the rejection of the representationalist "theory of ideas" (the veil of perception).

## Relations
Reid founds his philosophy explicitly to **react against** [David Hume](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/hume.md), refuting Humean skepticism. He **opposes** the immaterialism of [George Berkeley](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/berkeley.md) and the representative way of ideas in [John Locke](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/locke.md). He influenced the later Scottish school and 20th-century direct realism.
