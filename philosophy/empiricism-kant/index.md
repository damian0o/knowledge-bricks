# Empiricism & the Critical Turn

The British empiricists drive knowledge back to the senses, each pushing the previous one further, until Hume's skepticism dissolves causation and the self — and Kant answers by relocating structure into the mind itself.

## Concepts
* [Francis Bacon — Inductive Naturalism](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/bacon.md) - Reality is a mind-independent natural order uncovered by inductive experiment.
* [Thomas Hobbes — Corporeal Materialism](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/hobbes.md) - Reality is nothing but bodies in motion.
* [John Locke — Empiricism & the Veil of Ideas](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/locke.md) - A material world known only indirectly through ideas; primary vs. secondary qualities.
* [George Berkeley — Subjective Idealism](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/berkeley.md) - There is no matter; to be is to be perceived.
* [David Hume — Skeptical Empiricism](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/hume.md) - Causation, the self, and the external world cannot be demonstrated by reason.
* [Thomas Reid — Common Sense Realism](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/reid.md) - We perceive the external world directly; common sense needs no proof.
* [Immanuel Kant — Transcendental Idealism](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/kant.md) - We know phenomena structured by the mind, never the thing-in-itself. Grounds O1_FOUNDATION.
