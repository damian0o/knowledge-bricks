## The model of reality
Only body exists. Every phenomenon — perception, imagination, reasoning — is a motion of matter governed by mechanical causation. There is no immaterial substance; universals are merely names (nominalism), not real entities.

## Key concept
Corporeal materialism / mechanism — "all that exists is body" — combined with nominalism about universals.

## Relations
Hobbes was **influenced by** [Francis Bacon](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/bacon.md), for whom he worked as secretary. He **reacts against** the immaterial mind of [René Descartes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md), writing the Third Objections to the *Meditations* and rejecting substance dualism, and **opposes** Aristotelian Scholasticism. His mechanistic empiricism **influenced** [John Locke](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/locke.md).
