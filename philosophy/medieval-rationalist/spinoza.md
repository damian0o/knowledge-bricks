## The model of reality

There is only **one substance**, infinite and self-caused: **God, or Nature** (*Deus sive Natura*).
It has infinitely many attributes, of which we know two — **thought** and **extension**. Particular
minds and bodies are not substances but **modes**, finite modifications of the one substance. Mind
and body are the same reality expressed under two attributes (parallelism), and everything follows by
necessity from the divine nature; nothing is contingent.

## Key concept

Substance monism; *Deus sive Natura*; thought and extension as attributes; mind–body parallelism.

## Relations

He **refines** [Descartes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md)'s substance/attribute
apparatus but **reacts against** him by collapsing the plural substances into one. He **influenced**
[Leibniz](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md), who recoiled from the monism, and — much later
— the [German idealism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/hegel.md) of Hegel and Schelling, who found
in him the model of an all-encompassing absolute.
