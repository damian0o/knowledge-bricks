Philosophical Model of Reality

Augustine of Hippo — Christian Platonism

Reality is a hierarchy of being created ex nihilo and sustained by an immutable eternal God, in whom the eternal divine ideas reside and by whose light the mind knows truth.

  • #patristic
  • #christian-platonism

The model of reality

Reality is a graded order of being, created from nothing and held in existence by a wholly immutable, eternal God. The Platonic Forms become rationes aeternae — eternal reasons in the mind of God. Creatures are ranked by their degree of being, truth, and goodness; evil is privation, not a positive thing. The human mind grasps unchanging truths only by divine illumination.

Key concept

Divine illumination; the eternal reasons (rationes aeternae); creation ex nihilo.

Relations

He synthesizes Plato into Christian theology, drawing heavily on the emanation metaphysics of Plotinus (reworked so that the source is a personal creator God). He influenced Anselm and Aquinas, and his “si fallor, sum” (“if I am deceived, I exist”) influenced Descartes, anticipating the cogito.
