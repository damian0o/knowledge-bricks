## The model of reality

"Being" applies **univocally** to God and creatures — in one and the same sense, differing in degree
— rather than merely by analogy. Each individual is made *this* individual by an intrinsic positive
principle, **haecceity** (thisness), added to its common nature. Common nature and individual
difference are separated not really, nor merely in thought, but by a **formal distinction** — less
than real, more than conceptual.

## Key concept

Univocity of being; **haecceitas** (thisness); the formal distinction (*distinctio formalis*).

## Relations

He **reacts against** [Aquinas](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/aquinas.md), replacing the analogy of
being with univocity, and contends sharply with his contemporary Henry of Ghent. He **refines**
[Avicenna](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/avicenna.md)'s doctrine of common natures. He
**influenced** [William of Ockham](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/ockham.md), who would in turn
reject the common natures and the formal distinction.
