Philosophical Model of Reality

René Descartes — Substance dualism

Reality comprises three substances — infinite God and two finite created substances, thinking substance (mind) and extended substance (matter) — with mind and body really distinct.

  • #early-modern
  • #rationalism
  • #dualism

The model of reality

Starting from radical doubt, Descartes finds one indubitable point — cogito ergo sum — and rebuilds reality from it. There are three substances: infinite God, and two finite created kinds, res cogitans (thinking, unextended mind) and res extensa (extended, mechanical matter). Mind and body are really distinct. God, proven to exist and be no deceiver, guarantees that clear and distinct ideas are true.

Key concept

Cogito ergo sum; mind–body (substance) dualism; clear and distinct ideas; God as guarantor of truth.

Relations

He reacts against Scholastic Aristotelianism, replacing substantial forms with mechanism. His cogito was anticipated by Augustine, and he refines Anselm’s ontological argument. He influenced Spinoza, Malebranche, and Leibniz — and provoked the empiricist reaction of Locke, who denied his innate ideas.
