## The model of reality

Nothing in the world has causal power of its own. What looks like natural causation — fire burning
cotton — is really **God directly creating each event** in a habitual sequence. The connection
between "cause" and "effect" is not necessary but customary; God could suspend it at any moment.
Reality is therefore radically contingent on the divine will, and the philosophers err in treating
the cosmos as necessary or eternal.

## Key concept

Occasionalism; the critique of necessary causation; *The Incoherence of the Philosophers*.

## Relations

He **reacts against** [Avicenna](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/avicenna.md) — attacking emanation,
the eternity of the world, and the Necessary Existent reading — and opposes Aristotelian *falsafa*
generally. His attack provoked the reply of
[Averroes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/averroes.md). His occasionalism strikingly anticipates
that of [Malebranche](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/malebranche.md) and the later debate over
causation.
