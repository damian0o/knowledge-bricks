## The model of reality

Created things are composites of **essence** (what a thing is) and **esse** (its act of existence),
really distinct in everything except God. Substances are further analyzed by **act and potency** and
**form and matter**. God alone is **Ipsum Esse Subsistens** — subsistent existence itself — and all
beings exist by *participating* in his being. Being is spoken of by **analogy**, neither univocally
nor equivocally.

## Key concept

Real distinction between essence and **esse**; act and potency; God as *Ipsum Esse Subsistens*; the
analogy of being.

## Relations

He **synthesizes** [Aristotle](/philosophy/ancient/aristotle.md) with Christian theology and with
[Augustine](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/augustine.md), and **refines**
[Avicenna](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/avicenna.md)'s essence/existence distinction. He **reacts
against** [Averroes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/averroes.md) on the unicity of the intellect and
the demonstrable eternity of the world. His system provoked the critical responses of
[Duns Scotus](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/duns-scotus.md) and
[William of Ockham](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/ockham.md).
