Philosophical Model of Reality

Anselm of Canterbury — Necessary divine being

Reality is grounded in God as that than which nothing greater can be conceived, a supreme necessarily existing being whose existence is more real than any contingent thing.

  • #medieval
  • #scholasticism
  • #christian-platonism

The model of reality

At the foundation of reality stands God, defined as “that than which nothing greater can be conceived.” Such a being must exist not merely in thought but in reality, and necessarily — for necessary existence is greater than contingent existence. God is the most real being, the source of all goodness and being, and creatures hold reality by participation in him. Reason and faith cooperate: “faith seeking understanding.”

Key concept

The ontological argument; fides quaerens intellectum (faith seeking understanding).

Relations

He continues the Platonism of Augustine. His ontological argument was contested in his own day by the monk Gaunilo, and centuries later influenced Descartes, who revived it, and Leibniz, who sought to repair it.
