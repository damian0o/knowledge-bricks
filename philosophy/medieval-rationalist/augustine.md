## The model of reality

Reality is a graded order of being, **created from nothing** and held in existence by a wholly
immutable, eternal **God**. The Platonic Forms become **rationes aeternae** — eternal reasons in the
mind of God. Creatures are ranked by their degree of being, truth, and goodness; evil is privation,
not a positive thing. The human mind grasps unchanging truths only by **divine illumination**.

## Key concept

Divine illumination; the eternal reasons (*rationes aeternae*); creation *ex nihilo*.

## Relations

He **synthesizes** [Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md) into Christian theology, drawing heavily on
the emanation metaphysics of [Plotinus](/philosophy/ancient/plotinus.md) (reworked so that the source
is a personal creator God). He **influenced** [Anselm](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/anselm.md) and
[Aquinas](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/aquinas.md), and his "*si fallor, sum*" ("if I am deceived,
I exist") **influenced** [Descartes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md), anticipating the
cogito.
