## The model of reality

At the foundation of reality stands **God**, defined as "that than which nothing greater can be
conceived." Such a being must exist not merely in thought but in reality, and necessarily — for
necessary existence is greater than contingent existence. God is the most real being, the source of
all goodness and being, and creatures hold reality by participation in him. Reason and faith
cooperate: "faith seeking understanding."

## Key concept

The **ontological argument**; *fides quaerens intellectum* (faith seeking understanding).

## Relations

He continues the Platonism of [Augustine](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/augustine.md). His
ontological argument was contested in his own day by the monk Gaunilo, and centuries later
**influenced** [Descartes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md), who revived it, and
[Leibniz](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md), who sought to repair it.
