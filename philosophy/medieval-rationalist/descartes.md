## The model of reality

Starting from radical doubt, Descartes finds one indubitable point — **cogito ergo sum** — and rebuilds
reality from it. There are three substances: infinite **God**, and two finite created kinds,
**res cogitans** (thinking, unextended mind) and **res extensa** (extended, mechanical matter). Mind
and body are really distinct. God, proven to exist and be no deceiver, guarantees that **clear and
distinct ideas** are true.

## Key concept

*Cogito ergo sum*; mind–body (substance) dualism; clear and distinct ideas; God as guarantor of truth.

## Relations

He **reacts against** Scholastic Aristotelianism, replacing substantial forms with mechanism. His
cogito was anticipated by [Augustine](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/augustine.md), and he
**refines** [Anselm](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/anselm.md)'s ontological argument. He
**influenced** [Spinoza](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/spinoza.md),
[Malebranche](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/malebranche.md), and
[Leibniz](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md) — and provoked the empiricist reaction of
[Locke](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/locke.md), who denied his innate ideas.
