## The model of reality

Only **individuals** exist. Universals are not things in the world but names or concepts (*nomina*)
that signify many particulars; there is no shared "common nature" out there. Reality is parsimonious
— one should not multiply entities beyond necessity (**Ockham's razor**) — and radically contingent
on God's omnipotent will, so the created order could have been otherwise.

## Key concept

Nominalism; **Ockham's razor** (parsimony); divine omnipotence and contingency.

## Relations

He **reacts against** [Duns Scotus](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/duns-scotus.md) (denying common
natures and the formal distinction) and against the realist metaphysics of
[Aquinas](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/aquinas.md). His anti-realism about universals **opposes**
[Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md) most directly. His emphasis on individuals and experience
**influenced** the later nominalist and empiricist current that runs toward
[Hobbes](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/hobbes.md) and British empiricism.
