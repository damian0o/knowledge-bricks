## The model of reality

Malebranche keeps the Cartesian split of mind and body but denies that creatures have any real
**causal power**. Created things are only **occasions** on which **God**, the sole genuine cause,
acts. Likewise we do not perceive bodies directly: we **see all things in God**, perceiving the ideas
that exist in the divine understanding. Reality's order is thus continuously upheld by God's will and
made known through God's ideas.

## Key concept

**Occasionalism**; "**vision in God**" (we perceive ideas in God).

## Relations

He **synthesizes** [Descartes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md)'s framework with the
illumination theme of [Augustine](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/augustine.md), yet **reacts
against** Descartes by denying real mind–body interaction and creaturely causation (a position
paralleling [al-Ghazali](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/al-ghazali.md)). He **influenced**
[Leibniz](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md), who offered pre-established harmony as an
alternative, and the causation debates of
[Berkeley](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/berkeley.md) and [Hume](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/hume.md).
