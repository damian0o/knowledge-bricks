# Medieval & Rationalist Philosophy

From the theological hierarchies of being to the great rationalist systems — the long argument over
substance, God, mind, and matter that the empiricists and Kant would later contest.

## Theological foundations
* [Augustine of Hippo — Christian Platonism](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/augustine.md) - a created hierarchy of being sustained by an immutable God.
* [Anselm of Canterbury — Necessary divine being](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/anselm.md) - reality grounded in that than which nothing greater can be conceived.

## The Islamic and scholastic peak
* [Avicenna (Ibn Sina) — Necessary and contingent existence](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/avicenna.md) - the Necessary Existent and the essence/existence distinction.
* [Averroes (Ibn Rushd) — Purist Aristotelianism](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/averroes.md) - the eternal Aristotelian cosmos and shared intellect.
* [Al-Ghazali — Occasionalist contingency](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/al-ghazali.md) - no necessary causes; reality hangs on God's will.
* [Thomas Aquinas — Being and the act of existence](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/aquinas.md) - essence distinct from esse; God as subsistent existence.
* [John Duns Scotus — Univocity and haecceity](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/duns-scotus.md) - univocal being and individual thisness.
* [William of Ockham — Nominalism](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/ockham.md) - only individuals exist; universals are names.

## The rationalist systems
* [René Descartes — Substance dualism](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md) - reality split into thinking and extended substance, anchored by the cogito.
* [Nicolas Malebranche — Occasionalism and vision in God](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/malebranche.md) - God the sole cause; we see all things in God.
* [Baruch Spinoza — Substance monism](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/spinoza.md) - one infinite substance, God or Nature.
* [Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz — Monadology](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md) - reality is windowless monads in pre-established harmony.
