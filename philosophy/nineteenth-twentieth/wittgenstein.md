## The model of reality
**Early (Tractatus):** the world is the totality of facts, not things, and a meaningful proposition is a logical picture of a possible fact; what cannot be pictured must be passed over in silence. **Late (Investigations):** there is no single logical essence linking language to reality. Meaning is **use** within a "language-game," embedded in a form of life — the same word means different things in different practices, and context determines the concept.

## Key concept
The picture theory of meaning (Tractatus) giving way to meaning-as-use and language-games (Investigations).

## Relations
Wittgenstein was **influenced by** [Bertrand Russell](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/russell.md) and Frege in his early logical work, then **reacts against** his own earlier picture theory in the later philosophy. He **influenced** [Hilary Putnam](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/putnam.md) and the Vienna Circle.
