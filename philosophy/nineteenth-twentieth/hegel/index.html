Philosophical Model of Reality

G.W.F. Hegel — Absolute Idealism

Reality is Geist (Spirit) coming to know itself through a rational, dialectical process in history, so that the real is the rational and the rational is the real.

  • #german-idealism

The model of reality

Reality is not a fixed substance but a process: Geist (Spirit/Mind) unfolding and coming to self-consciousness through history by a dialectical movement in which each stage generates its own contradiction and is sublated into a higher synthesis. There is no inaccessible thing-in-itself behind appearances — the rational structure is the real.

Key concept

Absolute idealism; the dialectic (the unfolding of the Absolute through contradiction and sublation).

Relations

Hegel both refines and reacts against Immanuel Kant, rejecting the unknowable noumenon by absorbing it into the self-development of Spirit, and synthesizes Fichte’s idealism. He opposes Arthur Schopenhauer. He influenced Karl Marx and Friedrich Nietzsche.
