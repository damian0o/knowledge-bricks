Philosophical Model of Reality

Martin Heidegger — Fundamental Ontology

Reality is to be understood through the question of Being, disclosed primarily via Dasein's practical, temporal being-in-the-world rather than detached theoretical objects.

  • #20th-century

The model of reality

The tradition forgot the most basic question — the meaning of Being (Sein) itself — by treating reality as a collection of present-at-hand objects. Heidegger begins instead from Dasein, the being for whom Being is an issue, whose primary access to the world is practical, engaged, and temporal (being-in-the-world). Reality is first disclosed through use and care, not detached contemplation.

Key concept

The question of Being (Sein); Dasein and the ontological difference (between beings and Being).

Relations

Heidegger refines and then reacts against the phenomenology of his teacher Edmund Husserl, shifting from transcendental consciousness to existential being-in-the-world. He was influenced by Friedrich Nietzsche and Kierkegaard, and influenced Jean-Paul Sartre.
