## The model of reality
The tradition forgot the most basic question — the meaning of **Being** (Sein) itself — by treating reality as a collection of present-at-hand objects. Heidegger begins instead from Dasein, the being for whom Being is an issue, whose primary access to the world is practical, engaged, and temporal (being-in-the-world). Reality is first disclosed through use and care, not detached contemplation.

## Key concept
The question of Being (Sein); Dasein and the ontological difference (between beings and Being).

## Relations
Heidegger **refines** and then **reacts against** the phenomenology of his teacher [Edmund Husserl](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/husserl.md), shifting from transcendental consciousness to existential being-in-the-world. He was **influenced by** [Friedrich Nietzsche](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/nietzsche.md) and Kierkegaard, and **influenced** Jean-Paul Sartre.
