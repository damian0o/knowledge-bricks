## The model of reality
To get at reality rigorously we must suspend (the *epoché*) our naive assumption that the world simply exists "out there," and instead describe phenomena precisely as they present themselves to consciousness. Consciousness is always consciousness *of* something (intentionality), and the objective world shows up as the correlate of the intentional acts of a transcendental ego.

## Key concept
Phenomenology — intentionality and the epoché (transcendental reduction).

## Relations
Husserl **refines** the descriptive psychology of his teacher Franz Brentano and **reacts against** psychologism (the reduction of logic to empirical psychology). He was **influenced by** [William James](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/james.md). He **influenced** [Martin Heidegger](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/heidegger.md) and Maurice Merleau-Ponty.
