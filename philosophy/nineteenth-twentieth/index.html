19th–20th Century

After Kant, reality is reconceived again and again: as Spirit, as Will, as matter, as flux, as process, as logical structure, as language. The thread running to the present is that reality is increasingly bound up with mind, history, language, and the knowing community.

German idealism & its reactions

  • G.W.F. Hegel — Absolute Idealism - Reality is Spirit knowing itself dialectically through history.
  • Arthur Schopenhauer — The World as Will - Behind representation lies a blind striving Will.
  • Karl Marx — Dialectical Materialism - Material and economic conditions determine consciousness.
  • Friedrich Nietzsche — Perspectivism & Will to Power - No fixed facts, only forces and interpretations.

Process, pragmatism & phenomenology

  • Ernst Mach — Empiriocriticism - Reality is neutral sensations; absolute space is fiction.
  • Charles Sanders Peirce — Pragmaticism - The real is the long-run consensus of inquiry.
  • William James — Radical Empiricism - Reality is a stream of pure experience; truth is what works.
  • Henri Bergson — Duration - Reality is continuous creative duration grasped by intuition.
  • Edmund Husserl — Phenomenology - Describe phenomena as given to consciousness.
  • Alfred North Whitehead — Process Philosophy - Reality is momentary actual occasions in process.

Analytic & existential

  • Bertrand Russell — Logical Atomism - Reality is atomic facts mirrored by logical language.
  • Ludwig Wittgenstein — Picture Theory & Language-Games - Meaning is use within a language-game. Grounds O3_LINGUISTIC_SHIFT.
  • Martin Heidegger — Fundamental Ontology - Reality disclosed through the question of Being and Dasein.
  • W.V.O. Quine — Ontological Relativity & Holism - To be is to be the value of a bound variable. Grounds O4_RELATIONALIZATION.
  • Hilary Putnam — Internal Realism - Objects exist only relative to a conceptual scheme.
