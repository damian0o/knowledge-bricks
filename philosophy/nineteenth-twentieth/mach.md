## The model of reality
What is given is sensation — colours, sounds, pressures — the neutral "elements." Objects, the ego, atoms, and absolute space are not extra realities but mental economies: stable, useful groupings of elements. Concepts that can never be tied to observation (like Newton's absolute space and time) are metaphysical fictions to be discarded.

## Key concept
Elements / sensations and the "economy of thought"; Mach's principle — inertia is relational, determined by the distant masses of the universe.

## Relations
Mach **refines** the relationalism of [Gottfried Leibniz](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md) and **reacts against** the absolute space and time of [Isaac Newton](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/newton.md). He **influenced** [Albert Einstein](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/einstein.md) (Mach's principle and the critique of absolute frames), the Vienna Circle, [William James](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/james.md), and [Bertrand Russell](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/russell.md).
