## The model of reality
The world has a logical form that a properly analyzed language reveals: it is the totality of independent **atomic facts**, built from simple particulars and properties. In his later neutral monism, the apparent duality of mind and matter is dissolved — both are logical constructions out of a single neutral "stuff."

## Key concept
Logical atomism (and, later, neutral monism).

## Relations
Russell **synthesizes** mathematics and logic with [Alfred North Whitehead](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/whitehead.md) in *Principia Mathematica*, building on Frege. He **reacts against** the British idealism of F. H. Bradley, and his neutral monism was **influenced by** [Ernst Mach](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/mach.md). He **influenced** [Ludwig Wittgenstein](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/wittgenstein.md).
