## The model of reality
The real is that which inquiry, pursued far enough, would force any community of investigators to converge upon — the "final opinion." It is independent of what any one person thinks, yet not independent of thought as such. Generals and laws (universals) are themselves real (scholastic realism), not mere names.

## Key concept
The pragmatic maxim and pragmaticism — meaning and reality fixed by the ideal end of inquiry.

## Relations
Peirce **refines** [Immanuel Kant](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/kant.md) and **influenced** [William James](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/james.md) — though he later renamed his own view "pragmaticism" to **react against** James's looser version. He **influenced** [Hilary Putnam](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/putnam.md).
