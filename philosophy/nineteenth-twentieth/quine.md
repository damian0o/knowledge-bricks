## The model of reality
There is no first philosophy standing outside science. Our ontology — what we take to be real — is whatever our best total theory commits us to: "to be is to be the value of a bound variable." Beliefs do not face experience individually but as one interconnected **web**; any statement can be held true if we adjust others, so no belief is in principle immune from revision and none is purely analytic.

## Key concept
Ontological relativity ("to be is to be the value of a bound variable"); confirmation holism; the rejection of the analytic/synthetic distinction.

## Relations
Quine **refines** and **reacts against** the logical positivism of Rudolf Carnap, rejecting the analytic/synthetic dogma while keeping a naturalized empiricism. His holism descends from Pierre Duhem, and his pragmatism from [William James](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/james.md). He **influenced** Donald Davidson.

## Grounds
Quine grounds **[O4_RELATIONALIZATION](/eos/operators/o4-relationalization.md)**: epistemic holism — no belief or concept exists in isolation; each is a node in a web, and changing one propagates through the whole. The operator maps a concept `C → graph(relations(C))`: a bugfix is never just a bugfix, but a node in a graph.
