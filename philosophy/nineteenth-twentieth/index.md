# 19th–20th Century

After Kant, reality is reconceived again and again: as Spirit, as Will, as matter, as flux, as process, as logical structure, as language. The thread running to the present is that reality is increasingly bound up with mind, history, language, and the knowing community.

## German idealism & its reactions
* [G.W.F. Hegel — Absolute Idealism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/hegel.md) - Reality is Spirit knowing itself dialectically through history.
* [Arthur Schopenhauer — The World as Will](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/schopenhauer.md) - Behind representation lies a blind striving Will.
* [Karl Marx — Dialectical Materialism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/marx.md) - Material and economic conditions determine consciousness.
* [Friedrich Nietzsche — Perspectivism & Will to Power](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/nietzsche.md) - No fixed facts, only forces and interpretations.

## Process, pragmatism & phenomenology
* [Ernst Mach — Empiriocriticism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/mach.md) - Reality is neutral sensations; absolute space is fiction.
* [Charles Sanders Peirce — Pragmaticism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/peirce.md) - The real is the long-run consensus of inquiry.
* [William James — Radical Empiricism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/james.md) - Reality is a stream of pure experience; truth is what works.
* [Henri Bergson — Duration](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/bergson.md) - Reality is continuous creative duration grasped by intuition.
* [Edmund Husserl — Phenomenology](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/husserl.md) - Describe phenomena as given to consciousness.
* [Alfred North Whitehead — Process Philosophy](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/whitehead.md) - Reality is momentary actual occasions in process.

## Analytic & existential
* [Bertrand Russell — Logical Atomism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/russell.md) - Reality is atomic facts mirrored by logical language.
* [Ludwig Wittgenstein — Picture Theory & Language-Games](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/wittgenstein.md) - Meaning is use within a language-game. Grounds O3_LINGUISTIC_SHIFT.
* [Martin Heidegger — Fundamental Ontology](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/heidegger.md) - Reality disclosed through the question of Being and Dasein.
* [W.V.O. Quine — Ontological Relativity & Holism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/quine.md) - To be is to be the value of a bound variable. Grounds O4_RELATIONALIZATION.
* [Hilary Putnam — Internal Realism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/putnam.md) - Objects exist only relative to a conceptual scheme.
