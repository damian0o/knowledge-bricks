## The model of reality
Putnam moved from metaphysical realism — the "God's-eye view" of a ready-made world — to **internal realism**: the question "what objects does the world contain?" only has an answer relative to a conceptual scheme. Truth is not correspondence to mind-independent facts but idealized rational acceptability. Yet meaning is not purely subjective: "meanings ain't in the head" — reference is fixed partly by the external world and the community (semantic externalism).

## Key concept
Internal realism; the brain-in-a-vat argument; semantic externalism ("meanings ain't in the head").

## Relations
Putnam **reacts against** metaphysical realism and, in his internal-realist phase, **opposes** the thoroughgoing naturalism of [W.V.O. Quine](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/quine.md). He was **influenced by** the pragmatism of [William James](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/james.md) and [Charles Sanders Peirce](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/peirce.md), and by the later [Ludwig Wittgenstein](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/wittgenstein.md); his semantic externalism developed alongside Saul Kripke.
