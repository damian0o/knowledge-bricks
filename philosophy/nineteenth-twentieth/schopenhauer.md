## The model of reality
The world we experience is representation — appearance structured by the perceiving subject. But its inner essence, the thing-in-itself, is a single non-rational, purposeless **Will** that objectifies itself in everything. Reality is thus driven from below by blind striving, not from above by reason.

## Key concept
The World as Will and Representation — the Will as the metaphysical ground of appearance.

## Relations
Schopenhauer **refines** [Immanuel Kant](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/kant.md), identifying the thing-in-itself with the Will. He **opposes** [G.W.F. Hegel](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/hegel.md) and rationalist optimism. He **influenced** [Friedrich Nietzsche](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/nietzsche.md), [Henri Bergson](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/bergson.md), and the early Wittgenstein.
