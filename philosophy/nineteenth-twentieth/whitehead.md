## The model of reality
The basic units of reality are not enduring substances but **actual occasions** — momentary events of experience that arise, "prehend" (grasp) their predecessors, and perish into the data for new occasions. Reality is therefore through-and-through process and becoming; treating abstract enduring objects as concrete is the "fallacy of misplaced concreteness."

## Key concept
Process and actual occasions (prehension; the fallacy of misplaced concreteness).

## Relations
Whitehead was **influenced by** [Henri Bergson](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/bergson.md) and [William James](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/james.md), and **synthesizes** the logical work of his *Principia Mathematica* collaborator [Bertrand Russell](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/russell.md) into a speculative metaphysics. He **opposes** scientific materialism.
