Philosophical Model of Reality

Henri Bergson — Duration (Durée)

Reality is continuous, qualitative duration — an indivisible creative flow of time and becoming grasped by intuition rather than the spatializing intellect.

  • #20th-century
  • #process-philosophy
  • #vitalism

The model of reality

Real time is not the spatialized, measurable time of physics but durée — lived, continuous, qualitative duration in which past and present interpenetrate. The intellect cuts this flow into static, spatial units for practical use, but only intuition grasps reality as the indivisible creative movement it is.

Key concept

Duration (durée) and élan vital — reality as creative becoming.

Relations

Bergson was influenced by William James (mutual influence) and reacts against Herbert Spencer’s mechanistic evolution. He opposes Albert Einstein on the nature of time (their 1922 debate). He influenced Alfred North Whitehead.
