Philosophical Model of Reality

Alfred North Whitehead — Process Philosophy

Reality is composed not of static substances but of momentary "actual occasions" of experience in continuous process, related by prehension.

  • #20th-century
  • #process-philosophy

The model of reality

The basic units of reality are not enduring substances but actual occasions — momentary events of experience that arise, “prehend” (grasp) their predecessors, and perish into the data for new occasions. Reality is therefore through-and-through process and becoming; treating abstract enduring objects as concrete is the “fallacy of misplaced concreteness.”

Key concept

Process and actual occasions (prehension; the fallacy of misplaced concreteness).

Relations

Whitehead was influenced by Henri Bergson and William James, and synthesizes the logical work of his Principia Mathematica collaborator Bertrand Russell into a speculative metaphysics. He opposes scientific materialism.
