## The model of reality
There is no "true world" of fixed facts standing behind appearance — that idea is itself a life-denying fiction. Reality is a dynamic flux of competing forces, and every account of it is an interpretation from some perspective. At bottom, this striving is **will to power**.

## Key concept
Perspectivism and will to power (with the thought of eternal recurrence).

## Relations
Nietzsche was **influenced by** [Arthur Schopenhauer](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/schopenhauer.md) but **reacts against** his pessimism and resignation, affirming life and striving instead. He also **reacts against** the rational teleology of [G.W.F. Hegel](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/hegel.md). He **influenced** [Martin Heidegger](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/heidegger.md) and [Henri Bergson](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/bergson.md).
