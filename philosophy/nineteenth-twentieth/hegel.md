## The model of reality
Reality is not a fixed substance but a process: Geist (Spirit/Mind) unfolding and coming to self-consciousness through history by a dialectical movement in which each stage generates its own contradiction and is sublated into a higher synthesis. There is no inaccessible thing-in-itself behind appearances — the rational structure *is* the real.

## Key concept
Absolute idealism; the dialectic (the unfolding of the Absolute through contradiction and sublation).

## Relations
Hegel both **refines** and **reacts against** [Immanuel Kant](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/kant.md), rejecting the unknowable noumenon by absorbing it into the self-development of Spirit, and synthesizes Fichte's idealism. He **opposes** [Arthur Schopenhauer](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/schopenhauer.md). He **influenced** [Karl Marx](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/marx.md) and [Friedrich Nietzsche](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/nietzsche.md).
