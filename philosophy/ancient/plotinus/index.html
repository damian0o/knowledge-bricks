Philosophical Model of Reality

Plotinus — Neoplatonic emanation

Reality is a hierarchy emanating from a single ineffable source, the One, overflowing into Intellect, then Soul, then the sensible world, all proceeding from and returning to that unity.

The model of reality

All reality flows from one ineffable source, the One (the Good), which is beyond being and thought. From its superabundance it emanates in a descending order: Intellect (Nous, the realm of Forms), then Soul, then the sensible, material world at the lowest grade of being. All things proceed from the One and strive to return to it; matter is the faint, near-nothing edge of this procession.

Key concept

The One; emanation; the three hypostases (One — Intellect — Soul).

Relations

He refines Plato into a systematic metaphysics and synthesizes Aristotle (Nous as self-thinking thought) with the number-mysticism of Pythagoras. He reacts against the Stoics’ materialism and against the Gnostics. His hierarchy of being decisively influenced Augustine and the whole medieval Neoplatonic tradition.
