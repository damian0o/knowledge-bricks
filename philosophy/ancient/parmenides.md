## The model of reality

Reality is one — "what-is" — ungenerated, indestructible, indivisible, motionless, and complete.
Because "what is not" cannot even be thought or spoken, coming-to-be, perishing, and plurality are
incoherent; they belong only to the deceptive Way of Opinion, not the Way of Truth. Change is an
illusion; only changeless Being truly is.

## Key concept

"What is, is; what is not, is not." The split between the **Way of Truth** (Being) and the **Way of
Opinion** (the world of appearances and Becoming).

## Relations

He directly **opposes** [Heraclitus](/philosophy/ancient/heraclitus.md), for whom reality is flux —
the two together set the founding tension of Western metaphysics: Being versus Becoming. He
**reacts against** the Milesian physicists (Thales, Anaximander), who derived a changing cosmos from
a material principle. His doctrine **influenced** his pupil Zeno of Elea (whose paradoxes defend
monism) and decisively **influenced** [Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md), who took the
changelessness of true reality into the Forms, as well as the
[atomists](/philosophy/ancient/atomists.md), who answered him by splitting Being into many unchanging
atoms.
