## The model of reality

The skeptic makes no claim about what reality *is*. For every dogmatic assertion an equally strong
counter-assertion can be raised, so the wise response is **epoché** — suspension of judgment. We may
report how things *appear* ("the honey seems sweet") without committing to how they *are*. The
unexpected reward of giving up the search for hidden reality is **ataraxia**, tranquility.

## Key concept

**Epoché** (suspension of judgment); the skeptical modes/tropes; **ataraxia**.

## Relations

He transmits and systematizes the skepticism descending from Pyrrho of Elis. He **opposes** the
dogmatism of the [Stoics](/philosophy/ancient/stoics.md) and [Epicurus](/philosophy/ancient/epicurus.md)
alike, and **reacts against** the confident metaphysics of
[Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md) and [Aristotle](/philosophy/ancient/aristotle.md). His revived
arguments would later press hard on early-modern thinkers seeking certainty, including
[Descartes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/descartes.md).
