## The model of reality

Like the earlier atomists, Epicurus holds that only **atoms and void** exist and that the world
forms by natural processes, not providence. His key addition is the **clinamen** — an uncaused,
unpredictable atomic "swerve" — which breaks strict mechanical determinism and makes room for the
genuine novelty of compounds and for human freedom. The gods, if any, do not intervene.

## Key concept

Atoms, void, and the **clinamen** (swerve); a cosmos without providential design.

## Relations

He **refines** the [atomists](/philosophy/ancient/atomists.md) by inheriting their physics, yet
**reacts against** their strict determinism by introducing the swerve. He **opposes**
[Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md)'s immaterial reality and stands as the great rival of the
[Stoics](/philosophy/ancient/stoics.md), against whose providential, fated cosmos his chance-governed
universe is defined.
