## The model of reality

Ultimate reality is just **atoms and void** — countless indivisible, unchanging particles moving in
empty space. Macroscopic objects and all sensible qualities (color, taste, warmth) are not
fundamental but arise from the size, shape, arrangement, and collisions of atoms. The cosmos runs by
mechanical necessity, with no purpose or design.

## Key concept

"Atoms and void"; mechanistic necessity; qualities as derivative ("by convention").

## Relations

The atomists **react against** [Parmenides](/philosophy/ancient/parmenides.md) by splitting his single
unchanging Being into infinitely many unchanging atoms, so that plurality and motion (through the
void, a kind of "what-is-not") become possible. They **oppose** both
[Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md) (no immaterial Forms) and
[Aristotle](/philosophy/ancient/aristotle.md) (no immanent forms or final causes). Their materialism
**influenced** [Epicurus](/philosophy/ancient/epicurus.md), who adopted and modified the atomic
physics.
