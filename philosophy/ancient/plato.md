## The model of reality

What is fully real is the realm of **Forms** (Ideas) — eternal, unchanging, non-material universals
(the Good, Beauty, Justice, the mathematicals). The world we perceive is a changing, imperfect
copy: in the Allegory of the Cave, the senses show only shadows cast on a wall, while reason can
turn toward the source. Knowledge is of the Forms; mere opinion is of appearances.

## Key concept

The **Theory of Forms**, the Form of the Good, and the **Allegory of the Cave**.

## Relations

Plato **synthesizes** the two great predecessors: from [Parmenides](/philosophy/ancient/parmenides.md)
he takes the changelessness and full being of the truly real (the Forms), and from
[Heraclitus](/philosophy/ancient/heraclitus.md) the flux of the sensible world — assigning each its
own realm. He absorbs the mathematical metaphysics of
[Pythagoras](/philosophy/ancient/pythagoras.md), and **reacts against** the relativism of the
Sophists. He **influenced** his pupil [Aristotle](/philosophy/ancient/aristotle.md) (who would revise
him) and, centuries later, [Plotinus](/philosophy/ancient/plotinus.md), who systematized Platonism
into Neoplatonism.
