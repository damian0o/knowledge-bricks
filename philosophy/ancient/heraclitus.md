## The model of reality

Everything flows; nothing stays. Reality is process, not stasis — "you cannot step into the same
river twice." Yet the flux is not chaos: it is governed by the **Logos**, a rational measure and law,
and constituted by the strife and **unity of opposites** (day/night, war/peace). Fire is the
archetypal stuff, ever-living, kindling and going out in measures.

## Key concept

*Panta rhei* (everything flows); the **Logos** as cosmic ordering principle; the unity of opposites.

## Relations

He **opposes** [Parmenides](/philosophy/ancient/parmenides.md): where Parmenides denies change,
Heraclitus makes change the essence of reality — the two define the Being/Becoming axis. He
**influenced** [Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md) (whose sensible world of becoming echoes him)
and most directly the [Stoics](/philosophy/ancient/stoics.md), who adopted his Logos and his fire as
the active principle of the cosmos. His pupil Cratylus pushed the flux doctrine to the point of
silence.
