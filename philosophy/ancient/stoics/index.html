Philosophical Model of Reality

The Stoics — Corporeal cosmos governed by Logos

Reality is a single rational, providentially ordered material continuum pervaded by divine reason (Logos/pneuma), in which only bodies truly exist and all unfolds by fate.

  • #ancient
  • #stoicism

The model of reality

Only bodies are real (corporealism): even the soul, virtues, and God are subtle material. The cosmos is a unified, living continuum pervaded by pneuma — an active, rational divine breath (the Logos) — that organizes passive matter. Everything is bound by causal fate; the cosmos is providentially ordered and periodically renewed in eternal recurrence.

Key concept

Logos / pneuma; corporealism; cosmic determinism (fate); eternal recurrence.

Relations

They draw on Heraclitus, adopting his Logos and creative fire as the rational principle of the world. They react against Plato by rejecting incorporeal Forms, and oppose Epicurus — providence and fate against chance and swerve. Their pantheistic monism later influenced Plotinus, who nonetheless rejected their materialism.
