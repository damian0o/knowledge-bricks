## The model of reality

The real is the concrete individual **substance** (*ousia*) — this horse, this person — analyzed as a
union of **matter** (potentiality) and **form** (actuality). Forms are not in a separate world but
**immanent** in things. Change is the actualizing of potential, explained through the four causes
(material, formal, efficient, final), with an unmoved **Prime Mover** as the ultimate source of all
motion.

## Key concept

Substance (*ousia*); **hylomorphism**; actuality and potentiality; the four causes; the Prime Mover.

## Relations

He both **refines** and **reacts against** [Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md): he keeps form as
the key to reality but **rejects separate Forms**, pulling them down into individual things. He
**opposes** the [atomists](/philosophy/ancient/atomists.md), denying that mere matter-in-void could
explain form, purpose, and substance. His metaphysics later **influenced**
[Plotinus](/philosophy/ancient/plotinus.md), and through the medieval reception became the backbone
that [Avicenna](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/avicenna.md),
[Averroes](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/averroes.md), and
[Aquinas](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/aquinas.md) would build upon.
