## The model of reality

Only **bodies** are real (corporealism): even the soul, virtues, and God are subtle material. The
cosmos is a unified, living continuum pervaded by **pneuma** — an active, rational divine breath
(the **Logos**) — that organizes passive matter. Everything is bound by causal **fate**; the cosmos
is providentially ordered and periodically renewed in eternal recurrence.

## Key concept

**Logos / pneuma**; corporealism; cosmic determinism (fate); eternal recurrence.

## Relations

They draw on [Heraclitus](/philosophy/ancient/heraclitus.md), adopting his **Logos** and creative
fire as the rational principle of the world. They **react against** [Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md)
by rejecting incorporeal Forms, and **oppose** [Epicurus](/philosophy/ancient/epicurus.md) — providence
and fate against chance and swerve. Their pantheistic monism later **influenced**
[Plotinus](/philosophy/ancient/plotinus.md), who nonetheless rejected their materialism.
