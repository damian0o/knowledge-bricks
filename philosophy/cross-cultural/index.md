# Cross-Cultural — Non-Western Conceptions of Reality

Non-Western traditions that converge on — and often anticipate by millennia — the insights that ground EOS: that stability is *constructed*, not inherent, and that to exist is to exist in relation.

## Buddhist
* [Pratītyasamutpāda — Dependent Origination](/philosophy/cross-cultural/dependent-origination.md) - everything arises only in dependence on conditions; grounds O4_RELATIONALIZATION
* [Nāgārjuna — Śūnyatā (Emptiness)](/philosophy/cross-cultural/nagarjuna.md) - no thing has intrinsic essence; grounds the no-fixed-grounding axiom

## Vedānta
* [Śaṅkara — Advaita Vedānta](/philosophy/cross-cultural/advaita-vedanta.md) - only non-dual Brahman is real; the world is māyā

## Daoist
* [Laozi — The Dao as Nameless Ground](/philosophy/cross-cultural/laozi.md) - reality flows from an ineffable, nameless source
* [Zhuangzi — Dao as Transformation](/philosophy/cross-cultural/zhuangzi.md) - reality as ceaseless transformation; perspectives equalize
