Laozi — The Dao as Nameless Ground

Reality flows from the Dao, an ineffable nameless source prior to all distinctions, which generates and sustains the ten thousand things.

The model of reality

The Dao De Jing presents the Dao as the nameless origin “before Heaven and Earth” — that from which all things arise and to which they return. It cannot be fixed in language (“the Dao that can be named is not the eternal Dao”); naming and distinction are downstream of a reality prior to and beyond them. Things flourish by according with this spontaneous source (wu wei, non-forcing) rather than by imposing rigid order.

Key concept

The Dao · the nameless / ineffable ground · wu wei (non-forcing) · the unnameable prior to distinctions.

Relations

Its ineffable source beyond all naming parallels the One of Plotinus and the rational ground (Logos) behind Heraclitus’s flux; the limit of language anticipates the concerns of Wittgenstein (“whereof one cannot speak…”). Shares the Dao with Zhuangzi, stressing origin where Zhuangzi stresses transformation.
