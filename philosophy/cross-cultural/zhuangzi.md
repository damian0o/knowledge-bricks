## The model of reality

Zhuangzi (c. 4th c. BCE) sees reality as the Dao expressing itself as endless *transformation of things* (wù huà). Apparent fixed kinds and the rigid right/wrong of disputing philosophers are perspectival: each standpoint is partial, and the sage "walks two roads," letting distinctions equalize (qí wù). There is no privileged vantage from which to fix how things ultimately are; one moves with the flux rather than freezing it into categories.

## Key concept

The Dao · transformation of things (wù huà) · the equalizing of perspectives (qí wù) · spontaneity (ziran).

## Relations

A close non-Western kin of [Heraclitus](/philosophy/ancient/heraclitus.md)'s flux and of the process metaphysics of [Whitehead](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/whitehead.md) and the duration of [Bergson](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/bergson.md). Its refusal of a privileged standpoint anticipates [Nietzsche](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/nietzsche.md)'s perspectivism. Shares the Dao as ground with [Laozi](/philosophy/cross-cultural/laozi.md), stressing change where Laozi stresses origin.
