## The model of reality

A core doctrine of early Buddhism: "this being, that becomes; from the arising of this, that arises." Nothing has standalone existence; every phenomenon is conditioned by, and constituted through, its relations to others. Reality is a web of mutual conditioning rather than a collection of independent substances — which dissolves both an eternal self (anattā) and self-standing things into processes-in-relation.

## Key concept

Pratītyasamutpāda (dependent co-arising) · conditioned existence · the relational web · anattā (no fixed self).

## Relations

Provides the ground that [Nāgārjuna](/philosophy/cross-cultural/nagarjuna.md) radicalizes into emptiness. As a thoroughly relational ontology it resonates with [Quine](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/quine.md)'s holism (no belief or thing in isolation), [Latour](/philosophy/poststructuralist/latour.md)'s actor-networks, and [Whitehead](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/whitehead.md)'s mutually prehending occasions.

## Grounds

A ~2,000-year-old statement of [O4_RELATIONALIZATION](/eos/operators/o4-relationalization.md): a concept is what its relations make it — `C → graph(relations(C))` — long before Quine or Latour gave it modern form.
