## The model of reality

Śaṅkara (c. 700 CE) systematized Advaita ("not-two") Vedānta: ultimate reality is Brahman — one undifferentiated, changeless consciousness. The manifold world and the separate individual self are *māyā* — not sheer illusion, but appearance dependent on ignorance (avidyā), superimposed on Brahman as a snake is mistakenly seen in a rope. At the highest truth (pāramārthika) there is only Brahman; the everyday world holds at a lower, practical level (vyāvahārika).

## Key concept

Advaita (non-dualism) · Brahman · māyā / avidyā · the two levels of truth · Ātman = Brahman.

## Relations

A non-Western counterpart to the appearance/reality split of [Plato](/philosophy/ancient/plato.md) and to the world-as-perception idealism of [Berkeley](/philosophy/empiricism-kant/berkeley.md); its single changeless ground echoes the One of [Plotinus](/philosophy/ancient/plotinus.md) and the unchanging Being of [Parmenides](/philosophy/ancient/parmenides.md). It opposes the no-ground emptiness of [Nāgārjuna](/philosophy/cross-cultural/nagarjuna.md).
