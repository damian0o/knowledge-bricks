Philosophical Model of Reality

John Stewart Bell — Nonlocality

No theory can be both local and reproduce all quantum predictions, so reality is genuinely nonlocal — distant correlations cannot be reduced to pre-existing local properties.

The model of reality

Bell turned interpretation into experiment. His theorem shows that any theory recovering all quantum predictions must violate locality: distant entangled correlations cannot be explained by local pre-existing properties (local hidden variables). The world, then, is genuinely nonlocal. Bell personally admired Bohm’s realist programme and was sharply critical of Copenhagen’s vagueness about measurement.

Key concept

Bell’s theorem and Bell inequalities; the impossibility of local hidden-variable theories.

Relations

Bell refines Bohm’s hidden-variable programme and reacts against Bohr’s Copenhagen interpretation. His theorem opposes Einstein’s hope for local realism — refuting the local hidden variables of the EPR kind even while sharing Einstein’s realism.
