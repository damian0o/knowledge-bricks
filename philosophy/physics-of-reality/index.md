# Physics of Reality

Physicists treated as philosophers of reality — the arc from a single absolute classical stage to
a relative, observer-entangled, probabilistic world, ending at the still-open measurement problem.

## Concepts
* [Isaac Newton — Absolute Space and Time](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/newton.md) - one fixed, absolute, deterministic stage
* [Albert Einstein — Relativity](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/einstein.md) - no absolute frame, yet reality stays objective
* [Niels Bohr — Complementarity (Copenhagen)](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohr.md) - no observer-independent quantum reality
* [Werner Heisenberg — Uncertainty and Potentia](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/heisenberg.md) - reality as potentia actualized on measurement
* [Erwin Schrödinger — Wave Mechanics and the Cat](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/schrodinger.md) - the deterministic wavefunction and the measurement problem
* [Hugh Everett III — Many Worlds](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/everett.md) - a never-collapsing universal wavefunction that branches
* [David Bohm — Pilot-Wave and the Implicate Order](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohm.md) - deterministic, nonlocal, undivided wholeness
* [John Stewart Bell — Nonlocality](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bell.md) - no local theory can reproduce quantum predictions
* [The Measurement Problem — Quantum Interpretations](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/measurement-problem.md) - the open edge: what is the wavefunction, and how do outcomes arise?
