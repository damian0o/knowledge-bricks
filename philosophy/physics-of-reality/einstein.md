## The model of reality
Einstein removes Newton's absolute stage: there is no privileged frame, and simultaneity,
duration, and length depend on the observer's state of motion. Yet beneath this relativity lies
something objective and frame-independent — the geometry of spacetime itself. Reality, for
Einstein, exists independently of whether it is observed; this realist conviction drives his
lifelong resistance to the quantum orthodoxy.

## Key concept
Relativity of simultaneity; four-dimensional spacetime; mass–energy equivalence; later, "local
realism" and the EPR "elements of physical reality".

## Relations
Einstein **reacts against** [Newton's absolute space and time](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/newton.md).
He **synthesizes** [Mach's relational, anti-absolutist empiricism](/philosophy/nineteenth-twentieth/mach.md)
and [Leibniz's relational space](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md) into a positive
physical theory. He **opposes** [Bohr's Copenhagen interpretation](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohr.md)
in the Bohr–Einstein debates, insisting reality has definite properties independent of measurement.
