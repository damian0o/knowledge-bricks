## The model of reality
This is not a settled position but the live fault line where the conception of reality currently
stands. Quantum mechanics predicts superpositions, yet measurements yield single definite
outcomes. Why and how? Is the wavefunction a real physical thing (ontic) or only our information
(epistemic)? Competing answers — Copenhagen, many-worlds, hidden-variables, objective collapse,
QBism, relational and decoherence-based accounts — disagree on whether reality is
observer-dependent, branching, deterministic-but-nonlocal, or irreducibly indeterminate. The
matter remains open.

## Key concept
The measurement problem; the ontic vs. epistemic status of the wavefunction.
