## The model of reality
Newton treats space and time as a fixed, real backdrop that exists independently of the matter
within it — absolute, true, and mathematical, the same for all observers. Bodies have absolute,
true motions relative to this stage, and everything proceeds by deterministic mechanical laws.
This is the picture of an objective reality that observation does not alter.

## Key concept
Absolute space and absolute time; absolute (true) motion; deterministic universal mechanics.

## Relations
Newton **opposes** [Leibniz's relational view of space and time](/philosophy/medieval-rationalist/leibniz.md)
— the Leibniz–Clarke correspondence is the classic confrontation between substantival and
relational space. His absolute framework is the view that later **influences and provokes**
[Einstein's relativity](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/einstein.md), which dismantles it.
