David Bohm — Pilot-Wave and the Implicate Order

Reality is a deterministic, undivided whole in which particles always have definite positions guided by a real pilot wave, and the manifest world unfolds from a deeper implicate order.

The model of reality

Bohm restores determinism and definite properties: particles always have positions, guided by a real “pilot” wave (the quantum potential). The price is explicit nonlocality. More deeply, Bohm holds that the manifest, separable (explicate) world is an unfolding of a deeper, undivided (implicate) order — reality is fundamentally whole, and separateness is derivative. (His pilot wave revives an idea originally due to Louis de Broglie.)

Key concept

The pilot wave and quantum potential; the implicate and explicate order; undivided wholeness.

Relations

Bohm reacts against Bohr’s Copenhagen interpretation and refines Einstein’s hope for a realist, definite-property theory. His work directly influences Bell.
