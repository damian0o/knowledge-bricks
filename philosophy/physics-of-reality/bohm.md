## The model of reality
Bohm restores determinism and definite properties: particles always have positions, guided by a
real "pilot" wave (the quantum potential). The price is explicit nonlocality. More deeply, Bohm
holds that the manifest, separable (explicate) world is an unfolding of a deeper, undivided
(implicate) order — reality is fundamentally whole, and separateness is derivative. (His pilot
wave revives an idea originally due to Louis de Broglie.)

## Key concept
The pilot wave and quantum potential; the implicate and explicate order; undivided wholeness.

## Relations
Bohm **reacts against** [Bohr's Copenhagen interpretation](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohr.md)

definite-property theory. His work directly **influences** [Bell](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bell.md).
