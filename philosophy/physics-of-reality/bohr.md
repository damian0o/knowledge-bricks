## The model of reality
For Bohr there is no sharp line between the observed system and the observing apparatus; the
"phenomenon" is the indivisible whole of both. Mutually exclusive descriptions — wave and
particle, position and momentum — are **complementary**: each is valid in its experimental
context, none captures an observer-independent reality. Quantum properties are not possessed
prior to measurement.

## Key concept
Complementarity; the indivisibility of the quantum phenomenon; the inseparability of system and apparatus.

## Relations
Bohr **opposes** [Einstein's realism](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/einstein.md) in the
Bohr–Einstein debates. He **influences and synthesizes with**
[Heisenberg](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/heisenberg.md) to form the Copenhagen
interpretation. He is the figure that [Schrödinger](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/schrodinger.md),
[Everett](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/everett.md), [Bohm](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohm.md),
and [Bell](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bell.md) each **react against**.
