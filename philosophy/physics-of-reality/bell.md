## The model of reality
Bell turned interpretation into experiment. His theorem shows that any theory recovering all
quantum predictions must violate locality: distant entangled correlations cannot be explained by
local pre-existing properties (local hidden variables). The world, then, is genuinely nonlocal.
Bell personally admired Bohm's realist programme and was sharply critical of Copenhagen's
vagueness about measurement.

## Key concept
Bell's theorem and Bell inequalities; the impossibility of local hidden-variable theories.

## Relations
Bell **refines** [Bohm's hidden-variable programme](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohm.md) and
**reacts against** [Bohr's Copenhagen interpretation](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohr.md). His
theorem **opposes** [Einstein's](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/einstein.md) hope for *local*
realism — refuting the local hidden variables of the EPR kind even while sharing Einstein's realism.
