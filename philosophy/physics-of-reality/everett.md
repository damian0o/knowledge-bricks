## The model of reality
Everett removes collapse entirely. There is one universal wavefunction evolving deterministically
by the Schrödinger equation; measurement is just ordinary entanglement between system and
observer. Every possible outcome is realized, the world branching into many coexisting, mutually
unobservable copies. Probability becomes a matter of self-location among the branches rather than
of physical indeterminacy.

## Key concept
The universal wavefunction; the relative-state formulation; many worlds; no observer-induced collapse.

## Relations
Everett **reacts against** [Bohr's Copenhagen collapse](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohr.md)
and **refines** [Schrödinger's deterministic wavefunction](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/schrodinger.md)
by taking it with full ontological seriousness. He **opposes**
[Heisenberg's](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/heisenberg.md) actualization-on-measurement.
