## The model of reality
Heisenberg holds that conjugate quantities like position and momentum cannot simultaneously
possess definite values; the act of measurement irreducibly defines and disturbs the system.
Before measurement a quantum system exists as *potentia* — a realm of tendencies and
possibilities — actualized into a definite outcome only when observed. Reality at this scale is
genuinely indeterminate, not merely unknown.

## Key concept
The uncertainty (indeterminacy) principle; matrix mechanics; reality as potentia.

## Relations
Heisenberg **synthesizes with and influences** [Bohr](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohr.md)
to build the Copenhagen interpretation. He **opposes** [Einstein's](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/einstein.md)
demand for definite observer-independent properties.
