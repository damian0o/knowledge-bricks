## The model of reality
Schrödinger's wave mechanics describes the quantum state as a continuous wavefunction evolving
deterministically by his equation. But he rejected the Copenhagen claim that superpositions
persist into the macroscopic world: his cat, both alive and dead until observed, was meant to
expose the absurdity and to force the question of how and when a definite outcome arises.

## Key concept
The wavefunction and the Schrödinger equation; "Schrödinger's cat" and the measurement problem.

## Relations
Schrödinger **reacts against** [Bohr's Copenhagen interpretation](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/bohr.md)
and **refines** the formalism alongside [Heisenberg](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/heisenberg.md)
(wave vs. matrix mechanics, later shown equivalent). His continuous, collapse-free wavefunction
**influences** [Everett's many-worlds formulation](/philosophy/physics-of-reality/everett.md).
