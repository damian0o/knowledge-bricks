Philosophical Model of Reality

Isaac Newton — Absolute Space and Time

Reality is an objective, mind-independent stage of absolute space and absolute time in which bodies move under deterministic universal mechanical laws.

  • #classical-physics
  • #absolutism
  • #substantivalism
  • #mechanism

The model of reality

Newton treats space and time as a fixed, real backdrop that exists independently of the matter within it — absolute, true, and mathematical, the same for all observers. Bodies have absolute, true motions relative to this stage, and everything proceeds by deterministic mechanical laws. This is the picture of an objective reality that observation does not alter.

Key concept

Absolute space and absolute time; absolute (true) motion; deterministic universal mechanics.

Relations

Newton opposes Leibniz’s relational view of space and time — the Leibniz–Clarke correspondence is the classic confrontation between substantival and relational space. His absolute framework is the view that later influences and provokes Einstein’s relativity, which dismantles it.
