Philosophical Model of Reality

Niels Bohr — Complementarity (Copenhagen)

There is no observer-independent quantum reality with definite properties; what is real is constituted only within the indivisible whole of a phenomenon including its classical measuring apparatus.

  • #quantum
  • #copenhagen

The model of reality

For Bohr there is no sharp line between the observed system and the observing apparatus; the “phenomenon” is the indivisible whole of both. Mutually exclusive descriptions — wave and particle, position and momentum — are complementary: each is valid in its experimental context, none captures an observer-independent reality. Quantum properties are not possessed prior to measurement.

Key concept

Complementarity; the indivisibility of the quantum phenomenon; the inseparability of system and apparatus.

Relations

Bohr opposes Einstein’s realism in the Bohr–Einstein debates. He influences and synthesizes with Heisenberg to form the Copenhagen interpretation. He is the figure that Schrödinger, Everett, Bohm, and Bell each react against.
