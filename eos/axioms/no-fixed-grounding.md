## Statement

Concepts have no fixed grounding. A concept's content is not given by an external authority or a final definition; it is whatever operators do to it within a context.

## What it does NOT mean

This is **not** relativism. It means **local stability is constructed, not inherited** — the agent must build stability through operator composition (starting with [O1_FOUNDATION](/eos/operators/o1-foundation.md)) rather than assume it from outside.

## Historical resonance

This axiom is not new. [Nāgārjuna](/philosophy/cross-cultural/nagarjuna.md)'s śūnyatā (~150 CE) — that nothing has *svabhāva*, an intrinsic essence — states it some 1,850 years earlier: stability is conventional and constructed, never inherent.
